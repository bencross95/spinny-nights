Songs from the basement, The Old England, etc.

Norman

Formed amongst the forces of Bristol’s unorthodox music scene, Norman developed a taste for sounds that are equally unnerving and emotive. The band wrap classic songwriting in a new-age anxiety, fashioning ballads that find themselves halfway between mid-century jazz and the jerky art-punk of the late 70s.

Barely a year in the making, Norman dropped their first release, a three track visual mixtape, at the start of Spring this year. ‘Songs from the basement, The Old England, etc.’ captures a band in their first moments of creative content — the point at which they became confident in their craft.

Lynks Afrikka

A performer, an entrepreneur, and now a religion, Lynks Afrikka is the brainchild of Bristol based producer Elliot Brett. Lynks draws upon the legacy of 90s club-kid culture, merging music, drag and theatre in an unapologetically queer and utterly unique way.

The project was born out of Brett’s frustration with the current state of dance music; unsatisfied with the hyper-cool, straight-faced, fun-deprived club-nights now omnipresent, Lynks’ aim was singular — to bring pure unadulterated joy to its audiences.
